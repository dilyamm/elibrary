A Little Life

Hanya Yanagihara

Four college friends from a small Massachusetts school move to New York, driven by ambition and their deep bond, despite having little direction or money. Willem, JB, Malcolm, and Jude each pursue their dreams while their friendships evolve through challenges of addiction, pride, and success. At the heart of it all is Jude—brilliant yet deeply scarred by a traumatic past—whose unresolved pain threatens to shape all their lives.

The Bell Jar

Sylvia Plath

The Bell Jar chronicles the mental breakdown of Esther Greenwood: a young, brilliant, and talented woman who struggles with her identity and society's expectations. As she navigates her world, her emotional state deteriorates, plunging her into depression and disillusionment. Sylvia Plath’s haunting exploration of mental illness and the human psyche remains one of the most impactful works of American literature.

Schoolgirl

Osamu Dazai

Osamu Dazai's 1939 novella, Schoolgirl, explores a day in the life of a Tokyo schoolgirl and helped establish him as a literary figure in post-war Japan. Renowned for its ironic language and its depiction of an individual's struggle against societal norms, this new translation captures Dazai's unique style, offering a fresh perspective on one of Japan's most notable authors.
